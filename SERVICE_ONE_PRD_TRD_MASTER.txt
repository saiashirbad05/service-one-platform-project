======================================================================
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
======================================================================

Service-One is a web platform for checking whether a home appliance repair or installation quote is fair before the customer pays. The user enters location, appliance, service, and quoted price. Service-One then compares the quote against local market data, nearby provider data, historical community reports, trust signals, and brand-related service guidance.

The product promise is simple:

"Know the fair price before you pay."

The goal is to reduce overcharging, hidden charges, and low-trust mechanic selection in the Indian home appliance service market.

The selected product experience must feel:

- clean
- premium
- simple
- trust-first
- local
- human
- exact to the approved landing page style

This document combines both product requirements and technical requirements in one production-ready text file so the website can be designed, developed, tested, deployed, and scaled without ambiguity.

======================================================================
2. PRODUCT VISION
======================================================================

Service-One helps households make better service decisions for appliances by giving a fast, understandable, and trustworthy quote check before they commit to a payment.

The platform should become a trusted consumer utility for:

- AC installation and repair
- fridge repair
- washing machine repair
- TV repair
- RO service
- geyser repair or installation

Long-term, Service-One can become a broader household service intelligence platform, but the MVP must stay focused on appliance quote fairness.

======================================================================
3. PROBLEM STATEMENT
======================================================================

Users often receive repair or installation quotes without knowing:

- whether the quote is fair
- whether parts and labor are reasonably priced
- whether the provider is trustworthy
- whether the provider is using fake urgency or hidden charges
- whether better options exist nearby

As a result, users overpay, choose weak service providers, or delay important repairs because they do not trust the process.

14. RESULT AND VERDICT LOGIC
======================================================================

Verdict categories:

- fair: quote matches local range closely
- high: quote is above fair range but not extreme
- suspicious: quote is significantly above fair range or contains red flags
- low: quote is unusually low, possibly indicating quality concerns or fake estimates

Confidence levels:

- high: strong market data and multiple provider comparisons
- medium: limited market data
- low: insufficient local data

16. INTELLIGENT BACKEND DESIGN
======================================================================

The backend is powered by a multi-agent pricing intelligence system using ADK and Vertex AI. Agents work behind the scenes to combine pricing data, community data, brand guidelines, locality signals, and trust patterns.

Suggested agent roles:

1. Locality Price Agent
- fetches local market pricing signals for appliance and service

2. Provider Comparison Agent
- identifies nearby providers and approximate service charges

3. Community Report Analyzer Agent
- summarizes community-submitted quote reports for appliance and area

4. Trust and Red Flag Detector Agent
- checks for suspicious quote patterns or outliers
- checks official service guidance where relevant

5. Final Advisor Agent
- combines all signals into one result

Recommended model split:

- agents 1 to 4: Gemini Flash-Lite
- final synthesis agent: Gemini Flash

Reason:

- lower cost
- good speed
- enough quality for MVP
